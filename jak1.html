Synopsis


Jak and Daxter: The Precursor Legacy is the first of an immersive platforming trilogy. The series starts off in a cozy place called Sandover Village; home to our protagonists.

The story follows two friends, named Jak and Daxter. The two have a bad habit of not listening to their wise caretaker, Samos the Sage. Despite constant warning from the old man, Jak and Daxter "borrowed" the local fisherman's boat to venture to Misty Island; a dangerous place crawling with ravenous beasts known as lurkers.

After sneaking onto Misty Island the boys begin snooping around, when they stumble upon two villanous-looking people instructing a crowd of lurkers. After the crowd disperses, the boys continue exploring around and find a giant pool of dark-eco; a magical precursor essence which contains dark power. A lurker brute appears suddenly and lunges towards the boys.

Jak being the brave type he is, picks up a precursor relic and attempts to use it in self defense. Unfortunately, the blast launches him backward into Daxter, who is standing directly in front of the dark-eco pool. A few moments later and something emerges from the pool, but it doesn't look like Daxter...

What emerges from the pool of dark-eco certainly is Daxter, but he's a little bit shorter. Instead of coming back out normally, he returns from the pool in a new form; the form of an Ottsel. An Ottsel is a hybrid between an otter and a weasel, with orange and yellow fur.

The duo immediately starts panicking and promptly return home to Sandover Village. They go to the one person who may know a thing or two about this stuff; Samos. Unfortunately for the boys, not only do they get lectured by the sage, but Samos also admits that he doesn't know the full extent of power that dark-eco has.

Not all hope is lost, however. Samos begins to speak of someone who studies dark-eco and may know a way to return Daxter to his human form. Far, far to the north lives another sage; Gol Acheron, the dark-eco sage. Samos explains how Gol is the only one who would know how to revert the effects of the dark eco on Daxter.

From this moment on, the duo of Jak and Daxter embark on their long journey to far away lands, encountering other sages, monsters, and dangerous terrain. Crossing through jungles, mountains, volcanos, and oceans to reach this dark-eco expert.



Gameplay


Jak and Daxter: The Precursor Legacy has a very unique feel to it when it comes to gameplay. The premise of the game is platforming combined with eco power-ups, riding a giant land-bird, and even venturing through dangerous canyons and caverns on a hovering mechanical zoomer. The tricky thing about this game however, is you can't take that much damage before fainting. If you are at maximum health, you can only take damage a total of 4 times before fainting. This mechanic can be very unforgiving, but embraces the challenge of this type of game.

Eco

The first Jak and Daxter series revolves around energy sources called eco. There are 6 different types of eco in the Jak and Daxter universe.

The different types of eco are:

  • Green Eco - The eco of life. Used to heal and revitalize.

  • Blue Eco - The eco of speed. Symbolizes pure electricity and energy.

  • Yellow Eco - The eco of fire. Used to launch explosive projectiles.

  • Red Eco - The eco of strength. Used to give the player immense strength.

  • Dark Eco - The eco of destruction. Used for mutating and destroying.

  • Light Eco - The eco of purity. Used for healing and restoring balance.

Travel

In the original Jak and Daxter, there are two modes of travel for the player. Aside from the obvious main source being running on foot, there are two other methods. The first being a giant land-bird that you accidentally hatch at the beginning of the game. It gets all confused and assumes Daxter is it's "mama".

The Flut flut is a baby bird that travels quickly on land. Unfortunately, it's usage is restricted to closed off areas; so you can only ride it in those areas. Truly sad as it would be an incredible experience to explore the map while riding on that giant bird.

There are two locations that the player uses the Flut flut mount. The first being Boggy Swamp, near the Blue Sage's Hut. The area inside Boggy Swamp is closed off to where the Flut flut cannot leave. Although inside that area the bird is used to jump between elevated platforms throughout the swamp to avoid all the muck in the water.

The second area being Snowy Mountain, which is accessed via the Gondola near the Red Sage's Hut. In the Snowy Mountains, you use the Flut flut to glide across deep caverns to unlock the next area inside Snowy Mountain.

The second mode of travel the player uses is the hovering zoomer. A mechanical vehicle innovated and designed by Keira, daughter of Samos the Green Sage.

Keira spent a lot of time working on this "A-Grav Zoomer". Jak and Daxter's plan was to travel north to find a sage to return Daxter to normal. But just as their journey was beginning, there was something standing in the way; Fire Canyon. It is a volcanic pass consumed by lava and fire while also being the only path to Rock Village from Sandover Village.

After collecting enough power cells, Keira was able to equip the zoomer with a heat shield to withstand the scorching temperatures of Fire Canyon; allowing passage to Rock Village.

The zoomer, similarly to the Flut flut, is only usable in closed off areas. It's usable in Fire Canyon, Misty Island, Precursor Basin, and Mountain Pass.


Bosses


Surprisingly, there are only 3 actual boss encounters in the first Jak and Daxter game. As stated previously, you can only be a hit a few times before fainting; making some of these boss fights frustrating and challenging. The first boss encounter we're going to cover is the Dark-Eco Plant.

Dark Eco Plant

The Dark Eco Plant boss is hidden deep within the Precursor Temple of the Jungle next to Sandover Village. After venturing deep into the temple and hopping different platforms, you arrive at the plant's chamber.

The first thing to know about this boss is it's main attack. The boss will often try to lunge it's mouth at the player in attempt to eat them. Next, the boss will send little minions out at the player; these minions temporarily have spikes which can hurt the player. The player must defeat these to progress in the encounter.

Once the player defeats these minions, the boss will aim it's head upward and sprout leaves that the player can climb. This is the only time you can strike the boss dealing damage; when you are at the top of it next to it's face. Repeat this 3 times and the boss will be conquered.

In summary, the player has to follow these steps in order to win this fight:

  1. 1. Dodge the plant's lunge attacks

  2. 2. When the minions spawn, wait for their spikes to disappear before attacking them

  3. 3. When the boss sprouts leaves climb to the top next to it's face and attack

  4. 4. Repeat these steps 3 times until the boss is down

Klaww

The next boss in this game, known as Klaww, resembles a giant lurker with mechanical armor that launches molten rocks at the player. Immediately after completing the story around the blue sage's lab, Keira uses the blue sage's device to destroy the giant boulder blocking the path to the mountain pass.

However, before you can reach Mountain Pass, you must get past Klaww. The premise of this fight is quite unique, in that you have 3 small rock platforms to avoid projectiles on. Once you avoid enough of them, a ball of blue eco spawns. The blue eco empowers you to automatically build a platform which lets you get closer to Klaww, and once you deal damage to Klaww you must run away from a large boulder he launches at you until you get back to the rock platforms.


Similarly to the Dark Eco Plant, the player must follow these steps to win the encounter with Klaww:

  1. 1. Jump between rock platforms to avoid projectiles

  2. 2. Once blue eco spawns grab it to build the platform

  3. 3. At the end of the platform grab yellow eco to fire at Klaww

  4. 4. Run away from the giant boulder back toward rock platforms

  5. 5. Repeat 3 times

Precursor Robot

The final boss of The Precursor Legacy is Gol & Maia's Precursor Robot. The villainous duo has been working on this robot for quite some time in an attempt to bust open a dark eco silo, to cover the world in dark eco.

This boss fight is easily the most challenging throughout the first game. It involves many different mechanics and is very easy to slip up on.

The fight starts out with Jak and Daxter landing on the dark eco silo. You grab the nearby yellow eco to fire projectiles at the Robot, triggering the fight. After that, the Robot starts powering up a dark eco blast. A blue eco vent appears which allows you to use the nearby launch pad to propel yourself far into the air to avoid the blast.

The next step in the fight is once you land, the Robot summons dark eco-infused lurkers from the silo, which you can defeat with the nearby yellow eco. Once again the robot launches a blast which you avoid by jumping into the air on the jump pad.

After that, a new mechanic appears where the Robot places orange explosives on the silo. Each one blows up and creates a wave that the player must jump over to avoid taking damage. After you avoid all the waves you can grab yellow eco and fire at the boss.

The Robot's next mechanic is that it charges up those orange explosives in it's arm, creating a larger explosive projectile that the player has to dodge. The player is able to fire yellow eco at the boss while avoiding the charged-up blasts.

At this point in the fight is where the power of the four sages is combined to merge each type of eco into a pure form of eco, known as light eco. A ball of light eco will be thrown somewhere on the platform, and once it comes in contact with the player, a cutscene triggers where the player defeats the final boss.

This boss has many mechanics, so to sum up what you must do:

  1. 1. Fire yellow eco projectiles at the boss when possible

  2. 2. Use blue eco vents to power up the jump pad when the robot is about to blast the whole platform

  3. 3. Use yellow eco to take down the minions from a range

  4. 4. When the boss throws orange explosives on the ground, time your jumps to be able to avoid all the explosive waves

  5. 5. When the Robot charges up big orange explosives, fire yellow eco projectiles while it charges up, and dodge when the blast comes toward you

  6. 6. When the four sages eco powers are combined, wait for the light eco ball to land on the platform and make contact with it to win the fight

Once you finish the last step, a cutscene will trigger that wraps up the fight and completes the game. All in all, this boss can be super challenging for a lot of players and it's very easy to mess up and have to start over; but the challenge of the Jak and Daxter games is what makes the series amazing.

Final Thoughts


Overall, this game is a core part of the gaming industry. While it was lightyears ahead of it's time, it still holds up today; something rare to see in modern games. The story of The Precursor Legacy may not be the most intricate, however the feel of the game is unmatched. It really delivers that nostalgic Jak and Daxter feel to it, even after playing the later series games. While there is surely more to come in the rest of the Jak and Daxter franchise, this game holds a special place in my heart. There is no feeling like that of not playing Jak and Daxter for a while, and loading up The Precursor Legacy to get that nostalgia trip.

My score: 8 / 10
